Private Yacht Charter in Boston: The Ultimate Guide

Everything you need to plan an unforgettable experience on the water

Published May 1, 2026 8 min read Guide

Boston Harbor has been the heart of New England maritime culture for nearly four centuries. Today, it offers one of the most spectacular private yacht charter experiences on the East Coast. Whether you're celebrating a milestone, hosting clients, or simply craving the freedom of open water, chartering a private yacht in Boston puts you at the helm of an extraordinary day.

At Atlantis Charter, we've spent over two decades helping thousands of guests discover the magic of Boston from the water. This comprehensive guide distills everything we've learned into one resource, so you can plan your perfect charter with confidence.

Why Choose a Private Yacht Charter in Boston?

Boston Harbor is unlike any other charter destination in the United States. Where else can you cruise past Revolutionary War landmarks, anchor off pristine island beaches, and return to a world-class dining scene - all in a single afternoon?

The harbor spans over 50 square miles of protected water, making it ideal for yacht charters of every kind. From the Inner Harbor's iconic skyline views to the 34 islands that dot the Outer Harbor, there is no shortage of destinations. The seasonal variety adds another dimension: spring brings migrating whales just offshore, summer delivers warm breezes and long golden evenings, and autumn paints the harbor in fiery color that reflects off the water.

A private charter gives you something no public cruise or ferry can offer: complete control. You choose the route, the pace, the music, the food, and the company. It is your floating venue, designed around your vision.

Types of Yachts Available

One of the most common questions we hear is "What kind of boat should I choose?" The answer depends on your group size, the experience you want, and your budget. Here is a breakdown of the main categories in our fleet:

Motor Yachts

Motor yachts are the most popular choice for private charters. They offer spacious decks, climate-controlled interiors, and smooth rides even in moderate seas. Our fleet ranges from sleek 40-foot sport cruisers ideal for intimate groups of 6-10, up to 120-foot mega yachts that accommodate 100+ guests with full bar service, dance floors, and multiple lounging areas. Motor yachts are perfect for corporate events, yacht parties, and sunset cruises.

Sailing Yachts

For those who love the romance of wind-powered travel, our sailing fleet delivers an authentic experience. Sailing yachts range from classic 35-foot sloops to 80-foot luxury schooners. They are ideal for romantic getaways, small group celebrations, and anyone who appreciates the art of sailing. Learn more about the differences in our yacht vs. sailboat comparison guide.

Catamarans

Catamarans provide exceptional stability and space. Their twin-hull design means less rocking, making them excellent for guests who are new to boating or prone to seasickness. The wide beam creates expansive deck space, perfect for parties and events with 20-50 guests.

Fishing Boats

Our fishing fleet includes center-console boats, sport fishers, and deep-sea charter vessels equipped with top-of-the-line tackle and fish-finding electronics. Read our dedicated Boston fishing charter guide for species, seasons, and tips.

How Much Does a Yacht Charter in Boston Cost?

Charter pricing varies based on vessel size, duration, and season. Here is a general framework to help you budget:

  • Small vessels (30-45 ft, up to 10 guests): $800 - $2,500 for a half-day (4 hours)
  • Mid-size yachts (46-70 ft, 10-30 guests): $2,500 - $6,000 for a half-day
  • Large yachts (70-100 ft, 30-80 guests): $5,000 - $15,000 for a half-day
  • Mega yachts (100+ ft, 80-200 guests): $12,000 - $35,000+ for a half-day

Most charters include the captain, crew, fuel, and insurance. Catering, bar packages, entertainment, and decorations are typically add-ons that can be arranged through our concierge team. Need help choosing the right yacht size? We have a dedicated guide for that.

Planning Your Itinerary

Boston Harbor offers incredible variety within a compact area. A typical 4-hour charter can cover a surprising amount of ground. Here are some of our most requested routes:

The Skyline Cruise

Depart from Long Wharf, cruise past the Seaport District, loop around Fort Independence on Castle Island, and return via the waterfront with panoramic skyline views. This route is perfect for sightseeing groups and corporate entertaining.

Harbor Islands Explorer

Head out to Spectacle Island for swimming and beachcombing, continue to Georges Island to explore Fort Warren, and anchor off Lovells Island for a secluded lunch. Our Harbor Islands guide has everything you need to plan this route.

Sunset & City Lights

Time your departure for two hours before sunset. Cruise north past the Zakim Bridge, then south along the waterfront as the sun dips behind the skyline. Watch the city lights come alive from the water - an experience guests never forget.

What to Expect on Your Charter

Your charter experience begins before you even step aboard. Our team works with you to plan every detail, from the itinerary to any special requests. On the day of your charter:

  1. Arrival: Meet your captain and crew at the designated marina 15 minutes before departure.
  2. Safety briefing: Your captain provides a brief orientation covering safety equipment and vessel layout.
  3. Departure: Cast off and begin your adventure. The captain handles navigation while you enjoy the experience.
  4. On the water: Swim, fish, dine, celebrate, or simply relax. The agenda is entirely yours.
  5. Return: Arrive back at the marina refreshed and already planning your next charter.

Best Time to Charter in Boston

The Boston charter season runs from May through October, with peak demand in June, July, and August. Each month offers something unique:

  • May-June: Mild temperatures, fewer crowds, excellent fishing. Best for striped bass season.
  • July-August: Peak summer warmth, ideal for swimming and yacht parties. Book well in advance.
  • September-October: Spectacular fall foliage from the water, comfortable temperatures, bluefin tuna season. Many consider this the best time for a charter.
